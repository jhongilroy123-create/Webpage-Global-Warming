What is Global Warming?

Global warming is a gradual increase in the overall temperature of the earth's atmosphere generally attributed to the greenhouse effect caused by increased levels of carbon dioxide, chlorofluorocarbon (CFC), and other pollutants. It has far-reaching impact on biodiversity and climatic conditions of the planet. Several current trends clearly demonstrate that global warming is directly impacting on rising sea levels, the melting of ice caps and significant worldwide climate changes.

Why Does it Matter?

Global warming matters because it disrupts nature, melting ice, raising sea levels, and causing extreme weather. It threatens human health by worsening air quality, spreading diseases, and increasing heat-related illnesses. Food and water supplies are at risk as crops fail and water sources dry up. If left unchecked, it will endanger economies, communities, and the future of generations to come.
