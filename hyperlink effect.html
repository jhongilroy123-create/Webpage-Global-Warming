Rising Temperatures and Heatwaves

One of the most immediate effects of global warming is the steady rise in global temperatures. Heatwaves are becoming more frequent, longer, and more intense, putting vulnerable populations at great risk. This rise in temperature also worsens drought conditions in many parts of the world, leading to reduced water availability. Over time, prolonged exposure to extreme heat threatens agriculture, health systems, and even human survival in some regions.

Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels

Global warming is causing glaciers and polar ice sheets to melt at an alarming rate. This process contributes directly to rising sea levels, which endanger coastal cities and small island nations. Millions of people are at risk of becoming climate refugees as their homes are lost to the sea. Rising seas also lead to saltwater intrusion, contaminating freshwater supplies and damaging agriculture. If this trend continues, entire communities will face permanent displacement and global economic losses will soar.

Extreme Weather and Human Health

Climate change intensifies extreme weather events such as hurricanes, typhoons, floods, and wildfires. These disasters destroy homes, infrastructure, and livelihoods, causing billions in damages each year. At the same time, global warming worsens air quality, spreading diseases like asthma and other respiratory illnesses. Warmer temperatures also allow tropical diseases such as malaria and dengue to spread into regions where they were previously unknown. The combined impacts put a huge strain on healthcare systems, increase mortality rates, and threaten long-term human well-being.
